Have to and can

1. Have to for obligation

We can use have to and has to + infinitive to talk about obligation:

To score a goal you have to kick the ball into the other team's goal.
The batter has to hit the ball and run around the bases.

3. can for permission and can't for prohibition.

In tennis the ball can only bounce once.
Rugby players can't pass the ball forwards.
