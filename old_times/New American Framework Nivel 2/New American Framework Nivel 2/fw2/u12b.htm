Reading 2 for "Are You Sitting Comfortably?"

RSI is a common problem now for many people. It can happen to musicians and people who play lots of sports, for example. If you do a lot of work on the computer you could have problems.

RSI is caused by repeating the same movements many times. But it doesn't happen at once. Little aches and pains in the back, arms or wrist can be a sign that something is wrong.

There are lots of things you can do to prevent RSI. You should take breaks in your work and organize your office and desk so that you are comfortable. If you have aches and pains that persist for long periods of time you should go and see a doctor.

2. Are the following statements about modal verbs true or false?

Tip: Look at the text on RSI again.

  1. Modal verbs don't need an "s" when used in the third person. (he / she / it, for example.)

  2. As with other verbs, the auxiliary verb "do" is used to make a negative sentence with a modal verb.

  3. The auxiliary verb "do" is used to make a question with a modal verb.

  4. Modal verbs are followed by the infinitive without "to".

  5. If an adverb like always or also is used it is placed directly after the modal verb.

6.Talking about possibility—Might and could

Might and could are both used to say that something is possible.

  • This might not be good for her. = it is possible that this isn't good for her.
  • You could be experiencing the first symptoms. = it is possible that you are experiencing the first symptoms

    With positive sentences we can use either might or could. For example:

  • You could be experiencing the first symptoms.

    or

  • You might be experiencing the first symptoms.

    But with negative sentences we need to use might. For example:

  • This might not be good for her.

    not

  • This couldn't be good for her.
